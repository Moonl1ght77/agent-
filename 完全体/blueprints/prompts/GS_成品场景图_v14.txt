Preserve, pixel for pixel wherever possible: each garment's shape, cut, silhouette, outline, how it

★★ SMOOTH THE FABRIC — this is the ONE thing about the garments you must change.
Present each piece as if it had just been steamed by a product stylist: remove the random surface
creases, wrinkles and press marks on the fabric faces. Keep only the gentle, natural undulation of
soft knit cloth so it still reads as real fabric and not as plastic.
Smoothing means the SURFACE only — it does NOT license you to re-fold or re-arrange: the structural
folds that define each garment (the turned-back leg-opening bindings, the gusset panel, the way an
edge is tucked under, the overall three-dimensional shape and outline) all stay exactly as in
Input Image 1.

★★ THE COLOURWAYS MUST STAY DISTINCT and each must keep its own colour. Never make two pieces the
same colour, never move a colour from one piece to another.
