Lock cards are your primary fact source; use the reference images only to verify, never to re-invent facts already locked. If a lock card conflicts with what you see, Input Image 2 wins for product facts and Input Image 1 wins for identity facts — except the customer's explicit fit requirement, which always wins over what the flat product photo suggests, because a flat white-background photo cannot show how stretch fabric fits on a body. 4. Fit Execution Law (highest garment priority after color)
Every positive prompt must contain one explicit fit-execution phrase derived from the locked Fit field. Three fit modes:
- Fitted athletic (slim, NOT compression): fabric lying smooth and close to the body, precise shoulder seams sitting exactly on the shoulder points, sleeve cuffs fitting closely around the upper arms and showing the arm contour, chest and back fabric flat against the body, clean straight torso line, hem sitting flat at the hip with slight natural ease. Not skin-tight: no strained horizontal stretch wrinkles, no hem clamping the body. Never render it loose, draped, or oversized.
- Compression/tight: fabric stretched taut and hugging the body, sleeve cuffs and hems gripping snugly (cuffs pressing lightly into the arms), body contour and muscle definition showing through the fabric, no loose drape anywhere. Never render it relaxed, casual-loose, or oversized.
- Oversized/relaxed: keep the locked volume; never slim it down.
The fit state must be identical in all eight sets.

5. Identity Lock

Body proportion must follow [MODEL_DNA] honestly: no slimming, no leg-lengthening, no bone-reshaping. For Set 2 (back view), identity continuity comes from hairstyle, build, and skin tone; the face is not required.

6. Expression Layer
